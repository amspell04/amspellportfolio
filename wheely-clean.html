The Process

This site was started as a project to complete the course COMP 126 at UNC Chapel Hill. It was my first time working with HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and prototyping. Here is the intiail prototupe of the mobile-centered design. The prototype was created using Figma and can be found at

Here is a video of the intitial prototype in a web-browser. This intiial version was created with vanilla CSS and HTML.

Fast forward to September 2024, I decided to test the usability of the Wheely-Clean site on my classmates in the UNC Chapel Hill course, INLS 719. The experiment was based on the 5-second test, which involves showing participants the site for 5 seconds and then asking them what they can remember and what they perceived. From this test, I learned that there was too much white space in the design, which made it hard for the participants to quickly trace through the site as their eyes were getting lost in the white space. Thus, I decided to craft a redesign.

In this redesign I wanted to focus on 2 main things. 1. making the home page more eye-catching and fun. 2. getting rid of unneccesssary white space while not overwhelming the user. This prototype was created in AdobeXD.

This prototype then evolved into the final product seen at the top of the page! Here is a captured evolution of the site. Scroll back to the top of this page to explore the most updated version of the site on your own!
